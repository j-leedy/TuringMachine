Camera Mount and Setup

The camera mount was designed to be easily modified in terms of z-height in order to compensate for other changes in the design. It consists of three components: a 3D printed mount that holds the edges of the cameraâ€™s PCB, a pair of standoffs that can be swapped to change camera height, and a simple sheet metal shroud to prevent glare and improve consistency of bit-string recognition.

Box

The heart of our turing machine is the acrylic and Delrin box that houses our arduino, drive shafts, stepper motor, and tape actuation system. In order to optimize for a low budget, acrylic was chosen for all sides of the box save for the top because the top was the only face requiring post-processing following laser cutting. Using Delrin for the top allowed us to tap holes, making the assembly process much smoother. The box is mounted to a CNC-milled plywood base plate with precise locating features for the box, tape runner, and tape spools.
